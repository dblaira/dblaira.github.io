Pattern · Foreground / Background
Capacity is always foreground — it controls what renders. Move text is the signal. Category label and correlation chip are background support: they answer "why" after the move already made sense visually.
Center of Gravity
Stack: single card owns the frame — maximum decisional gravity. Queue: top item anchors, rest recede in opacity. Grid: 2-col-span hero pulls the eye; small cards are supporting context. All three resolve to "one top move" with different browse affordances.
Implied Interaction
Stack → swipe-to-dismiss, Tinder-style decisioning. Queue → tap to mark done, list collapses upward. Grid → tap to expand detail card, Pinterest-style. Capacity rail is shared across all three — same input, different output shape.
Capacity input: 3-state rail (Drained/Steady/Energized) or a continuous dial?
Move data: hardcoded set per category or AI-generated from Understood journal entries?
Ranking signal: pure correlation coefficient or also weighted by recent category gaps?
Completion: does finishing a move log to Supabase and feed back into the ontology?
Friction: should you need to declare capacity every session, or can it infer from recent check-ins?
